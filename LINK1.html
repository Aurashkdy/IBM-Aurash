NEPAL AND ITS HISTORY

Nepal’s history traces back to ancient times, beginning with early settlements in the Kathmandu Valley
and the birth of Siddhartha Gautama in Lumbini. The region evolved through the artistic and cultural
golden ages of the Licchavi and Malla dynasties before King Prithvi Narayan Shah unified the country
in 1769, establishing the modern Shah dynasty.

Never colonized, Nepal was governed for over a century by hereditary Rana prime ministers until a
democratic revolution in 1951. Following decades of political turbulence, a decade-long Maoist civil war
and a historic constituent assembly vote led to the abolition of the 200-year-old monarchy, culminating in
the establishment of a Federal Democratic Republic in 2008.
